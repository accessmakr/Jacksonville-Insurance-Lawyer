When You Need an Insurance Lawyer

Denied Claims

Insurers often cite obscure policy exclusions to deny legitimate claims. A lawyer forces them to justify the denial legally or face bad faith litigation.

Delayed Claims

Florida law imposes strict timelines on insurance payouts. Unreasonable stalling is a recognized tactic to exhaust claimants. Legal intervention resets the dynamic immediately.

Underpaid Settlements

Initial settlement offers are routinely calculated to be mathematically lower than your actual damages. Attorneys utilize independent adjusters and medical experts to demand fair compensation.

You should hire a lawyer immediately if your claim is denied, significantly underpaid, unreasonably delayed, or if the insurer claims your policy does not cover the incident despite clear evidence.
Most Jacksonville insurance lawyers work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they charge no upfront fees. They only get paid a percentage of the final settlement or court award if they win your case.
Bad faith occurs when an insurance company unreasonably delays a claim, fails to investigate properly, or denies coverage without a valid reason, breaching their duty to the policyholder.
